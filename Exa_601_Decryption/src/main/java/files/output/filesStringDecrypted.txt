The Caesar cipher is one of the oldest known encryption techniques.
It was named after Julius Caesar, who used it to protect his military messages.
This cipher works by shifting each letter in the plaintext by a fixed number of positions
down the alphabet.

For example, with a shift of 3, A becomes D,
B becomes E, and so on. The Caesar cipher is easy to understand
and implement but also easy to break with modern tools. Despite
its simplicity, it remains a great introduction to the concept of
encryption and helps demonstrate the basics of cryptographic thinking.
